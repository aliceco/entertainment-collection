Action

Inception (2010)

Dom Cobb is a skilled thief, the absolute best in the dangerous art of extraction, stealing valuable secrets from deep within the subconscious during the dream state, when the mind is at its most vulnerable. Cobb's rare ability has made him a coveted player in this treacherous new world of corporate espionage, but it has also made him an international fugitive and cost him everything he has ever loved. Now Cobb is being offered a chance at redemption. One last job could give him his life back but only if he can accomplish the impossible, inception. Instead of the perfect heist, Cobb and his team of specialists have to pull off the reverse: their task is not to steal an idea, but to plant one. If they succeed, it could be the perfect crime. But no amount of careful planning or expertise can prepare the team for the dangerous enemy that seems to predict their every move. An enemy that only Cobb could have seen coming.

Creator: Christopher Nolan

Actors: Leonardo DiCaprio, joseph Gordon-Levitt, Ellen Page, Tom Hardy, Ken Watanabe, Dileep Rao, Cillian Murphy, Tom Berenger, and more

Review

Close to a masterpiece by Nolan. Music, cinematography, acting, everything was brilliant. Just as we expect from one of the best. The fast moving plot was intriguing without being too much. It was one of those movies who left you with more questions at the end than answering questions. That might not be for everyone. But ff you don’t truly appreciate the complexity of the plot, you might appreciate the quality of everything else. Nolan collaborated as per usual with Hans Zimmer, who delivers a stunning film score. Overall a must see at least twice in your life time. Only thing the movie lacked is perhaps the character development part. Cobb had his development albeit small, but we know very very little about the rest of the cast which unfortunately lowers its all over score.

My rating: 9.8/10

Dunkirk (2017)

In 1940 during World War 2, thousands of Allied soldiers comprising of British and French troops, are stranded on Dunkirk - a French beach, and surrounded by Nazi Germans on land, sea, and air. They await evacuation while being picked off on all sides. The movie follows three narratives. Two soldiers who's trying to escape dunkirk (one week), a british private boat who's set to recue soldiers across the channel and three spitfire Pilots fighting the germans in the air space.

Creator: Christopher Nolan

Actors: Fionne Whitehead, Cillian Murphy, Tom Hardy, Harry Styles, Jack Lowden, Baryy Koeghan, Aneurin Barnard, Tom Glynn-Carney, James D'Arcy, and more.

Adventure

The Martian (2015)

During a manned mission to Mars, Astronaut Mark Watney is presumed dead after a fierce storm and left behind by his crew. But Watney has survived and finds himself stranded and alone on the hostile planet. With only meager supplies, he must draw upon his ingenuity, wit and spirit to subsist and find a way to signal to Earth that he is alive. Millions of miles away, NASA and a team of international scientists work tirelessly to bring "the Martian" home, while his crewmates concurrently plot a daring, if not impossible, rescue mission. As these stories of incredible bravery unfold, the world comes together to root for Watney's safe return.

Creator: Ridley Scott - based on the book by Andy Weir

Actors: Matt Damon. Jessica Chastain, Kristen Wiig, Jeff Daniels, Sean Bean, Kate Mara, Sebastian Stan, Aksel Hennie, Chiwetel Ejiofor, Benedict Wong, Mackenzie Davis, Donald Glover and more.

Review

The movie is good, but doesn’t quite live up to the expectations that the book set. The book is so focused on Marks own thoughts and experiences living on Mars and that is where the movie lacked. Matt Damon does a great job, but movie Mark just isn’t as funny as as book Mark. Marks humour got lost somewhere on the way to the drawing board which led the movie to just not reach the bar set by the book.

My rating: 7/10
