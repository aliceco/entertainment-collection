Action

La Casa de Papel (2017 - present)

Set in Madrid, a mysterious man known as "The Professor" recruits a group of eight people, who choose cities for code-names, to carry out an ambitious plan that involves entering the Royal Mint of Spain, and escaping with €2.4 billion. After taking 67 people hostage inside the Mint, the team plans to remain inside for 11 days to print the money as they deal with elite police forces. In the events succeeding the initial heist, the group are forced out of hiding and find themselves preparing for a second heist, this time on the Bank of Spain, as they again deal with hostages and police forces.

Creator: Álex Pina

Actors: Álvaro Morte, Úrsula Corberó, Itziar Ituño, Alba Flores, Jaime lorente, Pedro Alonso, Mugiel Herrán, Esther Acebo, Darko Perix, Hovic Keuchkerian, Paco Tous, Enrique Arce, and more.

Review

A quick paced heist show which just sucks you in. The show follows a group of criminals who have decided to pull off a big heist (later two). There are many parts of the show I love. Its characters, the acting, the music and how they show of the complexity of the heist. The show is hilt in a way to makes you really care for each and every main character. They all have their own reasons to join the heist, they all have their own history. They all come together and form a bond which further strengthen the charm of the show. The show also balances the thrill and action of the heist with more calmer bits and character development. It’s a show definitely worth watching, and for goodness sake watch it in Spanish with subtitles, to fully be able to appreciate the acting.

My rating: 8/10

Crime

Lucifer (2016 - present)

Lucifer Morningstar, bored from his sulking life in hell, comes to live in Los Angeles. While there, he helps humanity with its miseries through his experience and telepathic abilities to bring people's deepest desires and thoughts out of them. While meeting with a Detective in his nightclub (Lux), a shootout involving him and the Detective leads him to become an LAPD consultant who tries to punish people for their crimes through law and justice.

Creator: Tom Kapinos

Actors: Tom Ellis, Lauren German, Lesley‑Ann Brandt, D. B. Woodside, Rachel Harris, Aimee Garcia, Kevin Alejandro, and more.

Review

The best part of this show is Lucifer himself. Tom Ellis does such a great job portraying the narcissistic devil and brings the charm up an extra notch with his British accent. What the plot lacks, lucifers personality weighs up for. Personally I find the plot rather repetitive. Most episodes have a pretty clear formula it follows and although the show has a overarching plot it’s just not quite up there with some other shows. However it’s a good show if you want some good laughs, have a thing for narcissistic charming British people who occasionally sings, and just want some light to watch.

My rating: 6/10

Drama

Suits (2011-2019)

While running from a drug deal gone bad, brilliant young college dropout Mike Ross slips into a job interview with one of New York City's best legal closers, Harvey Specter. Tired of cookie-cutter law-school grads, Harvey takes a gamble by hiring Mike on the spot after recognizing his raw talent and photographic memory. Mike and Harvey are a winning team. Although Mike is a genius, he still has a lot to learn about law; and while Harvey might seem like an emotionless, cold-blooded shark, Mike's sympathy and concern for their cases and clients will help remind Harvey why he went into law in the first place. Mike's other allies in the office include the firm's best paralegal Rachel and Harvey's no-nonsense assistant Donna. Proving to be an irrepressible duo and invaluable to the practice, Mike and Harvey must keep their secret from everyone including managing partner Jessica and Harvey's archnemesis Louis, who seems intent on making Mike's life as difficult as possible.

Creator: Aaron Korsh

Actors: Gabriel Macht, Patric J Adams, Meghan Markle, Sarah Rafferty, Rick Hoffman, Gina Torres, and more.

Review

An absolutely great show if you witty comebacks and sarcastic humour. Its early seasons move in a slightly quicker pace, dealing with the troubles that the law firm a counter and winning cases. Although the plot itself in the long term gets a bit boring my love for the show comes from its characters. Harvey Specter and Mike Ross is an iconic duo with way too much ego for the own good, but there’s something that makes you still like them. The rest of the cast have their own quirks that makes you invested in their stories even if they’re just a side character, and that’s the beauty of the show.

My rating: 7/10
